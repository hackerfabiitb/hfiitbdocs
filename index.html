LITHOGRAPHY V0 POC

WHAT IS LITHOGRAPHY

Lithography [litho] is the machine that enables us to create really small patterns on wafers for making integrated circuits. It's performed after coating a film of photoresist on the wafer.

FIRST PRINCIPLES AND WORKING

  1. GOAL: Create physical patterns on a wafer
  2. KEY MATERIAL: Photoresist - a chemical whose solubility changes when exposed to UV light
  3. PROCESS: Expose certain areas to UV light, then dissolve in solvent to create patterns
  4. INNOVATION: Transform physical pattern problem into UV light pattern problem
  5. OUR APPROACH: DLP projector-based maskless lithography [no electron beam writer needed]

DLP TECHNOLOGY

WHAT IS DLP

Digital Light Processing uses a DMD [Digital Micromirror Device] chip containing tiny mirrors for each pixel [e.g., 1920x1080 mirrors for Full HD].

HOW IT WORKS

  • Each mirror can be individually controlled to be ON or OFF
  • Mirrors mechanically tilt toward the projection target [ON] or away [OFF]
  • RGB color wheel + Pulse Width Modulation = any color per pixel
  • Result: Precise, addressable pattern generation

WHY MASKLESS

Traditional lithography requires expensive masks made with electron beam writers - equipment most colleges don't have access to. Our DLP-based approach eliminates this requirement, making lithography accessible.

SPIN COATER V0 WIP

WHAT IS A SPIN COATER

A spin coater is a device used to deposit uniform thin films of photoresist or other materials onto substrates. It's an essential tool in semiconductor fabrication that comes before the lithography process.

FIRST PRINCIPLES AND WORKING

  1. GOAL: Create uniform thin films on wafer surfaces
  2. KEY PRINCIPLE: Centrifugal force spreads liquid evenly across rotating substrate
  3. PROCESS: Drop photoresist on wafer center, spin at high RPM [1000-8000], excess liquid flies off
  4. RESULT: Uniform coating thickness determined by spin speed and viscosity
  5. OUR APPROACH: Motor-based design with programmable speed control

WHY BUILD OUR OWN

Commercial spin coaters cost thousands of dollars and are often overkill for prototyping. A DIY spin coater can achieve similar results for photoresist coating at a fraction of the cost, using readily available motors and 3D printed parts.

KEY COMPONENTS

  • High-speed brushless motor [BLDC] or stepper motor
  • Motor controller with programmable speed
  • Vacuum chuck or mechanical chuck for wafer holding
  • Splash guard and collection container
  • Power supply
  • 3D printed mounting structure

DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS

  • SPEED CONTROL: Need precise RPM control for different coating thicknesses
  • VIBRATION: Minimize vibration for uniform coatings
  • SAFETY: Proper splash guards to contain flying photoresist
  • WAFER HOLDING: Secure mounting without damaging wafer
  • COST: Keep total cost under 10000 INR for V0
